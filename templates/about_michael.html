Ambition for Tyneside Software

Jobs are the objective — good ones if we can manage it. High-quality CVs for the people who work with us are a core priority.

A place to think differently

Through my own experience — and my children’s — I have some insight into the differing needs of neurodivergent people. We don’t discriminate: everyone is welcome. We are creating a space where we can be ourselves and think differently.

Start: a self-sustaining ecosystem

Initial plans centre on a self-sustaining loop around charity cleaning and field-service software: free tools for cleaners, paid work through welcome-home cleans, websites and junior roles funded by real commercial work ((jobs engine).

Train on the job

We try to give work to people just entering the workforce, or who may have no existing skills yet — training on the job and building practical experience they can carry for life.

Any problem, great or small

Our only limit is how much work we can achieve. We will apply ourselves to any problem great and small. We start with passions — data and logistics — and expand wherever the work takes us.

Sky is the limit

With extensive experience scaling software teams and delivering high-quality products, and a team with determination and desire to succeed — the sky is really the limit.

  • Document automation — workflows that turn paper/PDF into systems of record
  • Field services / logistics — scheduling, routing, work management for teams on the road
