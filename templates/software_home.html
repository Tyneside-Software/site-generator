Our vision

Through Michael’s own experience — and his children’s — we have insight into the differing needs of neurodivergent people. We don’t discriminate: everyone is welcome. We are creating a space where we can be ourselves and think differently.

Jobs for people at the start

We aim to give work to those just entering the workforce, or who may have no existing skills yet — training on the job and building practical experience that sticks.

CVs are a core priority

Jobs are the objective — good ones if we can manage it. Either way, high-quality CVs for our people are non-negotiable: real delivery, real references, real proof of work.

Any problem, great or small

Our only limit is how much work we can achieve. We will apply ourselves to any problem great and small. Start with passions — data and logistics — then expand wherever the work takes us.

Experience + determination

With deep experience scaling software teams and delivering high-quality products, and a team with the determination to succeed — the sky is really the limit.

How we create jobs in Tyneside

While we’re building core data and logistics products, our team also builds professional websites for local businesses and takes on field-service and automation work that funds junior roles.

Website revenue → junior roles

Every website for a paying customer funds real engineering jobs from the bottom up. People entering or re-entering work get trained on live client delivery. Every pound of that revenue recycles into more junior roles.

Our own sites are the proof

This site, the charity site, the cleaner portal — built in-house. Living proof of delivery, and a pathway from local talent to skilled engineers — one project and one strong CV at a time.

This is the jobs engine.

The three-pronged plan
